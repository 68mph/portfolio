Objectives:

With four locations, Brookhaven Opticians are one of the biggest full-service eye care centers on Long Island, NY. They were looking to update their onlinepresence with a new wesite to reflect the quality of their service. Their current website was outdated and not functional. With a difficult navigation and lack of relevent content, conversions were almost non-existent.

  • Improve overall aesthetic and user experience to make the site more engaging and to drive trust and credibility.
  • Provide vital relevant information and for website visitors.
  • Provide information about the staff, facility and therapy services. Expedite the patient registration process with downloadable insurance forms
  • Provide an aonline shop for customers to browse and purchase eyewear directly from their website.
  • Build a CMS simple enough for them to update and maintain basic content.

Scope:

  • Competitor research
  • User research
  • UI Design
  • Web Development
  • Client content administration
  • SEO strategy

Critical issues with original website:

Not only was the old site badly outdated in terms of design. Content was outdated and poorly structured. Lack of an SEO strategy left the wesbsite with no visibility in ranking.

  • Navigation is confusing and poorly structured.
  • Branding is overly dominant and takes up too much space.
  • Home page has no useful content or CTA's.
  • Sign ups and scheduling too diffucult to locate.
  • Footer is cluttered and diffucult to read.
  • Interior content is sparely populated and outdated.
  • Poor SEO with no keywording.
  • Poor user experience inhibited users form purchasing eyewear from their online store.

Information Architecture:

The client had three very clear servicess to provide through the website:

  • Provide eCommerce for users looking to purchase glasses online.
  • Help promote and schedule appointments for eye exams.
  • Provide full-service eye care for their kids brand "KIDS SPECS".

Using their extensive client hisotry, we established three sets of user personas.

  • Seniors looking for eye care and purchase glasses.
  • Young adults interested in teh latest brands and styles of glasses.
  • Children in need of full service eye care.
