HTML5 / CSS3 / WordPress / UI Design

Established almost a half century ago by Arsenio R. Mastrorocco in 1956, Brookhaven Opticians' mission statement and code of ethics is to provide quality precision eyewear with a concentration in filling difficult prescription eyewear, infant, and pediatric eyewear.

Objectives:

DSJCPA was unhappy with their current website provider and wanted to make some big changes. Their current site design was not clean and very cluttered. They wanted to move towards their branding and style guide and provide a more visually appealing look to their website.

  • Improve overall aesthetic and user experience to make the site more engaging and to drive trust and credibility.
  • Provide vital relevant information for website visitors.
  • Provide information about the staff, facility and therapy services. Expedite the patient registration process with downloadable insurance forms
  • Provide an online shop for customers to browse and purchase eyewear directly from their website.
  • Build a CMS simple enough for them to update and maintain basic content.

Analyze issues with original website:

Not only was the old site badly outdated in terms of design. Content was outdated and poorly structured. Lack of an SEO strategy left the website with no visibility in ranking.

  • Home page has no useful content or CTA's.
  • Color palette was not effective and did not reflect the brand.
  • Navigation was overly complicated
  • Stock imagery was not interesting and did not accurately represent the company.
  • Poor SEO results and ineffective keywords.
  • Bad conversion rate.

Research:

Using Miro, we created a white board to visualize and plan out the website development. All relevant information is gathered and placed in a clear, visual presentation. Information about the client, company, current website are present. Style guides, stock imagery and mood boards are all visible for clear interpretation. Clients, designers and copywriters can communicate and interact through the entire design and development process.

Information Architecture:

The client had three very clear services to provide through the website:

  • Promote and create CTA's for their top 4 servcices.
  • Promote the companys 3 verticals.
  • Add CTA for scheduling a consultation.
  • Display the latest news and social media entries.

Style Guide:

The client provided us with a style guide for the primary color palette and a preference for sans serif fonts. Monstserrat provided a great balance of weight and style to accompany the theme. We supplied a variety of secondary colors for the client, which paired nicely with the primary blue and orange.
