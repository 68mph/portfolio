SIGNAGE & VEHICLE WRAPS

East End Sign Designs

East End Sign Design has been has been creating quality signs here on Long Island since 1996. They provide custom products and services, including vehicle wraps & graphics, car decals & striping, truck lettering, and boat lettering & graphics.

Scope:

  • Competitor research
  • User research
  • UI Design
  • Web Development
  • Client content administration
  • SEO strategy

Objectives:

With a large shop and signage factory on Long Island's east end, the client came us us with no existing website. Aside from establishing an online presence, they had no real direction in terms of what they were looking for.

After using Google Analytics, SEO tools, and doing competitor research, we established a marketing scheme and developed the information architecture we felt would stimulate their business. This web design project was driven by content strategy: creating an engaging product and service site, and using their content for both search and user experience. We wanted to provide more opportunities for visitors to get to know East End Sign Designs and the interesting services and exciting projects they have completed.

  • Create an overall aesthetic and user experience to make the site engaging and to drive trust and credibility.
  • Provide vital relevant information for website visitors.
  • Provide information about the staff, facility and vehicle wrapping services.
  • Make it easy to place the users in contact with the sales department to get the information and pricing they need.
  • Build a CMS simple enough for te client to update and maintain basic content.

Research:

Using Miro, we created a white board to visualize and plan out the website development. All relevant informaton is gathered and placed in a clear, visual presentation. Information about the client, company, current website are present. Style guides, stock imagery and moodboards are all visible for clear interpretation. Clients, designers and copy writers can communicate and interact throught the entire design and development process.

Information Architecture:

The client had three very clear servicess to provide through the website:

  • Communicate the company's numerous services.
  • User photos of past work to illustrate their process and quality work.
  • Display a call to action ro direct user to inquiry form.

Style Guide:

The initial landing page drew visitors with robust graphics and CTA’s, to direct the user to relevant information points, services and inquiry forms and establish contact with the doctors and set appointments to expedite treatment.

I began establishing a color palette from basic branding styles and some examples the cliient gave us. The client preferred a darker theme. I paired the vibrant green of their logo with a dark nuetral color. This gave the website a feel of power, elegance, and formality. I established an orange companon Color for labels and highlights which worked well with the primary palette. This color guide gave room for the conpany's photo content, which includes a wide assortment of bright, powerful colors in their work, which inclues vehicle wrappings and storefront signage.
